About COVID-19

COVID-19, caused by the novel coronavirus SARS-CoV-2, was first identified in December 2019 in Wuhan, China. It rapidly spread around the globe, leading to a pandemic that affected millions of people.

The virus primarily spreads through respiratory droplets when an infected person coughs, sneezes, or talks. It can also spread by touching surfaces contaminated with the virus and then touching the face, especially the mouth, nose, or eyes.

Extra Info

Symptoms

Symptoms of COVID-19 can vary widely but commonly include:

  • Fever or chills
  • Cough
  • Shortness of breath or difficulty breathing
  • Fatigue
  • Muscle or body aches
  • Loss of taste or smell
  • Sore throat
  • Congestion or runny nose
  • Nausea or vomiting
  • Diarrhea

Prevention Measures

Preventive measures include:

  • Wearing masks in public spaces
  • Practicing physical distancing
  • Regularly washing hands with soap and water for at least 20 seconds
  • Avoiding close contact with sick individuals
  • Getting vaccinated when eligible

Resources

For more information and the latest updates, refer to trusted sources like the World Health Organization (WHO) and the Centers for Disease Control and Prevention (CDC).
